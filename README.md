# Online Retail - Customer Segmentation Analysis

This is one of my portfolio projects where I analyzed online retail transaction data to understand customer behavior and sales patterns. This was a fun project because I could directly see how the analysis can help real business decisions.


1.What the Project is About
I worked with a dataset of online orders and tried to answer important business questions like:
- Who are our best customers?
- How well are we retaining customers over time?
- Where should the marketing team focus their efforts?

2.What I Did
- Cleaned the messy raw data (removed cancellations etc.)
- Did exploratory analysis and created some visualizations
- Implemented RFM segmentation to group customers
- Ran Cohort Analysis to see retention trends
- Created clear business segments (Champions, At Risk, Lost, etc.)

3.Main Findings
- A small group of "Champion" customers brings in a big chunk of the revenue
- Retention is good in the beginning but drops quite a lot after 3 months
- There are many "At Risk" customers who used to buy but have stopped recently

4.Recommendations
- Start a loyalty program for the Champion customers
- Create special offers to bring back At Risk customers
- Focus more on keeping new customers engaged in their first few months

5.Tools I Used
- Python (Pandas, NumPy)
- Plotly and Seaborn for charts
- RFM and Cohort Analysis techniques
